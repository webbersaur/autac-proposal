General
A retractile cord (also called a coiled cord or curly cord) is an electrical cord that has been permanently formed into a helical coil shape. When stretched, it extends to several times its retracted length, then springs back to its original coiled form when released. This self-retracting design eliminates tangling, reduces trip hazards, and keeps workspaces organized.
They are different names for the same type of product — a helically coiled electrical cord that self-retracts. "Retractile cord" is the industry-standard UL term, "coiled cord" is commonly used in industrial and OEM contexts, and "curly cord" is the everyday term most people recognize from telephone handset cords. At Autac, we manufacture all three — because they're all the same thing.

Products & Specifications
Yes. Autac retractile cords are UL and CUL listed, meeting rigorous safety standards for the U.S. and Canadian markets. Our UL listings cover a wide range of cord types, gauges, and configurations. Specific UL file numbers are available upon request.
Autac offers retractile cords in several insulation materials:
  • PVC — Versatile, cost-effective, good chemical resistance
  • TPE (Thermoplastic Elastomer) — Flexible, RoHS compliant, wide temperature range
  • TPR (Thermoplastic Rubber) — RoHS-compliant alternative to Neoprene
  • Neoprene — Excellent for harsh environments, oil and weather resistant
Each material has specific advantages for temperature range, flexibility, chemical resistance, and compliance requirements. Use our Build Your Cord tool to explore options.
Autac manufactures retractile cords from 26 AWG (signal-level) up to 10 AWG (heavy power), with conductor counts ranging from 2 to 25+. Common configurations include 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 10, 12, 15, 18, 20, and 25 conductors. Shielded options are also available. Browse our product catalog for standard configurations.
A retractile cord typically extends to approximately 5 times its retracted (coiled) length. For example, a cord with a 2-foot retracted length will extend to roughly 10 feet. Custom extension ratios are available depending on the gauge, material, and coil diameter.
Yes. Autac manufactures shielded retractile cords for applications requiring electromagnetic interference (EMI) protection. Shielding options include spiral (serve) shields and braided shields. Shielded cords are commonly used in medical, telecommunications, and industrial instrumentation applications.
